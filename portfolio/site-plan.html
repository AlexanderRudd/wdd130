PURPOSE OF THE PORTFOLIO / BIOGRAPHY

My portfolio website will hold all of the future projects that I work on, either independently or with my team. It's a simple and easy place to view my experience. It also allows future employers to see my personality through my work. If there are collaborative projects on the site, they can reach out to those I worked with to get another witness of my teamwork ability, technical skill, and work ethic. The website also allows me to build a visual identity that becomes associated with my name. It will be easier to see patterns in my work and projects, allowing future employers or customers to know what they can expect. The site will hold everything a future employer would need to hire me, including business contact information and my resume. WHAT QUESTIONS WILL THIS SITE ANSWER? The website will serve to answer questions such as what kind of experience he has. They can go to the site and see that I've worked in Java, CSS, and other coding languages quickly and easily. Another question this site will answer is the quality of my work. Not only does it allow them to see it on the site, but they can also quickly research more about the associated projects online. As an example, if I worked on a specific game, they could research reviews of the game afterwards. They will be able to see a few select reviews I highlight on the site, but the simple transparency of the portfolio will allow them to quickly find other reviews. Another question they might ask is who I am. Like a typical resume, my portfolio will contain a small biography that allows them to answer questions like "What are his wants?" "What projects does he like to work on?", and "What is his personality like?" All of these questions can be quickly and easily answered through the various pages of my portfolio.

DESIGN OF PORTFOLIO / PERSONAL PROJECTS

The portfolio will be divided into several different pages. There will be a page with a biography, holding key information like my name, age, background, and personal desires. This allows the employer or visitor to quickly connect or find common ground with me, as well as understand who I am. Each large-scale project, like involvement in a AAA production or indie game, would be its own page, detailing how I contributed, as well as holding unique WIP pictures from the workflow process. In a team project, I would include reviews from my coworkers as well as things like professional reviews or critiques. Dividing each page into its own project allows for a simple overview that doesn't distract the viewer with an overflow of information. Smaller projects might be grouped 2-3 to a page, stopping the portfolio from having an excessive amount of pages. From a personal perspective, this allows me to record my projects and personal growth. I can see how my talents and skills have grown over the years, and I keep a record to show future friends and family. It's also something I could quickly share the link to on social media, allowing me to spread all of my work through a single link. A goal I would have for myself is to keep the website updated consistently with my projects, keeping myself accountable to keep working. Because it would host personal projects as well as ones from my work, it would motivate me to keep working on smaller personal projects. Though I currently don't have enough projects to fill up three pages, I want to set the personal goal of filling three pages by the end of the year. This site will help me remember this goal and have a concrete plan.
